Renewable Energy Projects in Australia

Australia has seen rapid growth in renewable energy projects over the past decade. This visualisation explores the distribution, growth, and types of renewable energy projects across the country. We will take a closer look at where these projects are located, how capacity has increased over time, and the proportion of energy types that make up Australia’s renewable energy landscape.

Geographic Distribution of Projects

The map below shows the geographical distribution of renewable energy projects across Australia. Each circle represents a project, with its size corresponding to the capacity (in megawatts) and its color indicating the type of energy (Solar, Wind, Hydro). Hover over each circle to see more details about the project, including its name and year of operation.

Growth of Renewable Energy Capacity Over Time

Renewable energy capacity in Australia has been growing steadily. The line chart below illustrates the cumulative capacity of renewable energy projects over time, segmented by energy type. This chart provides insight into which types of renewable energy have seen the most growth and which have emerged as the dominant sources of clean energy in recent years.

Proportion of Renewable Energy Types

Renewable energy in Australia is diverse, but some types are more prominent than others. The donut chart below shows the proportion of each energy type—solar, wind, and hydro—in the total renewable energy capacity. This helps us understand the overall contribution of each type to the country’s energy mix.

Cumulative Growth of Renewable Capacity by Energy Type

This stacked area chart shows the cumulative capacity growth of renewable energy projects over time in Australia. It helps identify how different energy types (Solar, Wind, Hydro) have contributed to the overall renewable energy mix over the years.

Total Investment Cost by Energy Type

The bar chart below illustrates the total investment costs for different types of renewable energy projects in Australia. It reflects the financial commitment to each type of energy and provides insight into which energy sources have received more economic backing.

CO2 Emission Reduction vs. Capacity

This scatter plot demonstrates the relationship between the capacity of renewable energy projects and the amount of CO2 emissions they reduce. It highlights how larger projects contribute significantly to reducing greenhouse gas emissions, underscoring the environmental impact of renewable energy in Australia.

This visualisation is created by Youttavong Chan. The data used is filtered from multiple files from the Australian Energy Regulator, Clean Energy Regulator, Australian Renewable Energy Agency, Global Power Plant Database, and National Greenhouse and Energy Reporting.
